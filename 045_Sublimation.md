# 045. Sublimation

## A First Look

Dry ice seems to smoke because solid carbon dioxide changes straight into gas. That unusual shortcut is sublimation.

In science, **sublimation** means **the change from solid directly to gas without becoming liquid first**. The word may seem simple at first, but it gives scientists a precise way to describe patterns in physical science.

## The Main Idea

A helpful way to remember sublimation is to think of it through the phrase **solid shortcut**. That phrase or image is not perfect, but it gives your mind a handle for the concept before the details become more formal.

Sublimation happens when particles leave a solid and become gas directly.

Dry ice, some air fresheners, and frost disappearing on cold dry days show sublimation.

## Common Misconception

Sublimation is not melting; no liquid stage appears during the change.

This matters because science rewards careful distinctions. Two ideas can sound similar in ordinary speech but mean different things in science class.

Use when creating `101_Climate_02.png` through `101_Climate_08.png`. Each file is referenced in `101_Climate.md` at the placement noted below.

**Style** (from `_create_more_images.md`): crude, funny, hand-drawn explainer cartoon; stick-figure characters; rough black outlines; mostly white background; selective flat accent colors. Labels, arrows, exaggerated faces, simple metaphors. Minimalist, humorous, concept-first, intentionally rough. Color sparingly: **red** = danger/extremes, **green** = climate/long-term growth, **blue** = weather/ocean/systems, **yellow** = warnings/sun, **gray** = bureaucracy/dull machinery. Vary panel width/height. Ages 11–13; sports travel, closets, maps, coaches — no political slogans.

## 101_Climate_05.png — Same average, different climates

**Placement:** End of "Averages Tell Part of the Story — Variability Tells the Rest" (after spread paragraph).

**Scene:** Two city skylines side by side, both thermometers reading **65°F AVERAGE**.

- **City A:** mild all year, tiny temperature swings, happy stick-figure in light jacket year-round
- **City B:** roasting summer sun, freezing winter snow, same average label — stick-figure sweating AND shivering in split panel
- Footer: **AVERAGE ALONE HIDES THE SWINGS**

**Colors:** Red summer; blue winter; green mild city.

**Aspect:** Wide (~2:1).

**Caption idea:** Same yearly average — totally different year-round feel.

## 101_Climate_08.png — Trend vs variability (warming ≠ never cold)

**Placement:** End of "Recent Change and Human Influence" (after trend + variability paragraph).

- Snowflake on one cold day with label **STILL WEATHER**
- Greenhouse gas icons (simple CO₂ molecules) with arrow **extra heat trapped**

**Aspect:** Wide (~2:1).

# 017. Gravity

## A First Look

Drop a pencil, and it falls. Jump, and you come back down. The Moon circles Earth, and Earth circles the Sun. All of these events are tied together by one of the grandest forces in nature: gravity.

In science, **Gravity is the attractive force between objects that have mass.** This idea helps us explain everyday events and also helps engineers design safer, smarter tools.

## The Main Idea

Gravity is the attractive force between objects that have mass. For this topic, the key relationship is:

**Weight = mass × gravitational field strength**

Scientists may write it as:

**W = m × g**

A common unit or measurement idea is the **newton (N) for weight or gravitational force**.

The important habit is to ask what is changing, what is staying the same, and how the measurement helps us compare one situation with another.

## Gravity Is Universal

Every object with mass attracts every other object with mass. You attract Earth, and Earth attracts you. Earth's pull is noticeable because Earth has enormous mass. The pull between two classroom objects is real but far too small to feel.

## Mass, Weight, and g

Mass is the amount of matter in an object. Weight is the force of gravity on that mass. On Earth, the gravitational field strength is about 9.8 newtons per kilogram. That means a 10 kg object weighs about 98 newtons on Earth.

## Falling and Orbiting

Gravity makes objects accelerate downward when air resistance is small. Gravity also keeps planets and moons in orbit. An orbit is like falling around a planet: the object keeps moving forward while gravity continually bends its path.

## Tides and Worlds

The Moon's gravity helps cause ocean tides on Earth. Gravity also shapes stars, planets, and galaxies. Without gravity, gas and dust would not gather into stars, and planets would not hold atmospheres.

## Gravity in Engineering

Engineers must account for gravity when building bridges, rockets, elevators, roller coasters, and sports equipment. A rocket needs tremendous thrust to overcome Earth's gravity and reach space.

## Why This Term Matters

A student who understands gravity can look at ordinary events with sharper eyes. He can explain why tools are shaped the way they are, why athletes move the way they do, and why engineers must think carefully before building vehicles, machines, buildings, and safety equipment.

This concept is also a bridge to later science. In high school and college, you will meet more exact mathematics, but the central idea will remain the same: careful definitions help us turn observations into explanations.

## Chapter Summary

- Gravity is an attractive force between masses.
- Weight is the gravitational force on an object's mass.
- On Earth, W = m × g is a useful formula for weight.
- Gravity causes falling, orbits, tides, and the formation of large structures in space.
- Gravity is essential in engineering and everyday life.

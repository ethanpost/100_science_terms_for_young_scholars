# Image briefs — 045 Shadow

Use when creating or updating `045_Shadow_01.png` through `045_Shadow_10.png`. Each file is referenced in `045_Shadow.md` at the placement noted below.

Match **040_Reflection** / **041_Refraction** style: clear labels, arrows for light rays, simple colors, print-friendly, ages 11–13. Avoid photorealism and cluttered text. Favor active, relatable scenes (recess, sports, flashlight) where appropriate.

## 045_Shadow_01.png — Shadow at recess (opening)

**Placement:** Top of chapter (after title).

**Scene:** Sunny school blacktop or field: student running or jumping; dark shadow on ground mirrors pose. Optional: sneakers, basketball court line. Keep focus on “dark copy” beside the real person—not a second body.

**Labels (optional):** Shadow — light blocked.

**Caption in chapter:** `![Shadow](045_Shadow_01.png)`

**Note:** Existing art may differ; align with opening hook (recess, shadow copies your move).

## 045_Shadow_02.png — Straight-line rays and shadow

**Placement:** End of “Light Travels in Straight Lines.”

**Labels:** Light ray; object blocks light; shadow.

**Caption idea:** Straight-line rays and a shadow.

**Placement:** End of “Blocking Light.”

**Labels:** Light source; object; shadow on wall.

## 045_Shadow_04.png — Sharp vs soft shadow

**Placement:** End of “Light Sources.”

**Scene:** Two small panels (or split image):

1. **Small/distant source** (dot Sun or tiny bulb) → sharp-edged shadow.
2. **Large/spread source** (big lamp or cloudy sky icon) → soft, fuzzy edge.

**Labels:** Sharp shadow; soft shadow; large source.

**Caption idea:** Sharp shadow vs soft shadow.

## 045_Shadow_06.png — Shadow size and distance

**Placement:** End of “Shadow Size.”

**Scene:** Side view: flashlight, hand or ball, wall. Two configurations or arrows:

- Object near light → large shadow on far wall.
- Object near wall → smaller shadow.

**Caption idea:** Shadow size and distance.

## 045_Shadow_07.png — Shadows during the day

**Placement:** End of “Shadows During the Day.”

**Scene:** Same stick figure or person at three times: morning (low Sun east, long shadow west), midday (high Sun, short shadow), afternoon (low Sun west, long shadow east). Simple Sun arc or three Sun positions.

**Labels:** Morning; midday; afternoon; Earth rotates (small note optional).

**Caption idea:** Morning, midday, and afternoon shadows.

## 045_Shadow_08.png — Sundial

**Placement:** End of “Sundials.”

**Scene:** Flat dial with hour marks; **gnomon** (triangular or stick) casting shadow across numbers. Sun in sky with one ray direction.

**Labels:** Gnomon; shadow; sundial.

**Caption idea:** Sundial with gnomon and shadow.

## 045_Shadow_09.png — Transparent, translucent, opaque

**Placement:** End of “Transparent, Translucent, and Opaque.”

**Scene:** One light source, three vertical panels in a row:

1. Clear glass — light passes; little or no shadow.
2. Frosted glass or wax paper — pale, blurry shadow.
3. Cardboard or wood — dark, sharp shadow.

**Labels:** Transparent; translucent; opaque.

**Caption idea:** Transparent, translucent, and opaque.

## 045_Shadow_10.png — Solar eclipse

**Placement:** End of “Solar Eclipses.”

**Scene:** Side view—not to scale: Sun, Moon between Sun and Earth, cone of shadow (umbra + penumbra) hitting Earth. Optional: tiny figure in umbra path.

**Labels:** Sun; Moon; Earth; Moon’s shadow; umbra (optional).

**Safety note for art:** Do not show anyone looking at the Sun without eclipse glasses.

**Caption idea:** Solar eclipse: Moon’s shadow on Earth.

## 037_Melting_02.png — Solid vs liquid particles

**Placement:** End of “Solids and Liquids.”

**Scene:** Side-by-side particle diagrams.

| Blue circles in a regular grid, small vibration marks | Same circles looser, motion arrows between neighbors |

## 037_Melting_03.png — Temperature during melting

**Placement:** End of “Temperature During Melting.”

**Scene:** Simple graph or beaker sequence.

- Beaker with ice and water; thermometer near **0 °C** flat while ice remains
- Red arrows labeled “thermal energy added” into the beaker

**Caption idea:** Temperature can stay nearly constant while ice melts.

## 037_Melting_04.png — Melting and freezing

**Placement:** End of “Melting and Freezing.”

**Scene:** One substance (water) at **0 °C** with two arrows:

- **Melting:** ice → water, arrow labeled “thermal energy added”
- **Freezing:** water → ice, arrow labeled “thermal energy removed”

Same temperature line between both paths.

**Caption idea:** Opposite changes at the same temperature for a pure substance.

## 037_Melting_05.png — Ice is less dense than water

**Placement:** End of “Water When Ice Melts.”

**Caption idea:** When ice melts, water takes up slightly less space per mass.

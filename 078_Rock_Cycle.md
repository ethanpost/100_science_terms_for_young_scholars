# 078. Rock Cycle

## A First Look

A rock may seem permanent, but Earth can melt, squeeze, break, bury, and rebuild it into something new.

In science, **rock cycle** means **the set of processes that change rocks from one type to another over time**. The word may seem simple at first, but it gives scientists a precise way to describe patterns in geology.

## The Main Idea

A helpful way to remember rock cycle is to think of it through the phrase **rock transformation**. That phrase or image is not perfect, but it gives your mind a handle for the concept before the details become more formal.

The three main rock types are igneous, sedimentary, and metamorphic.

Weathering, erosion, deposition, heat, pressure, melting, and cooling drive the cycle.

In modern science, rock cycle also connects to tools and decisions. Researchers may measure it directly, build models of it, compare examples of it, or use it to solve practical problems. Engineers, doctors, environmental scientists, astronomers, and chemists all depend on clear terms like this one.

## Common Misconception

The rock cycle is not a perfect circle with one required order; rocks can take many paths.

This matters because science rewards careful distinctions. Two ideas can sound similar in ordinary speech but mean different things in science class.

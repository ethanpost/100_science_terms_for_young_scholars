`081_Periodic_table_01.png` already exists at the chapter top (full table overview with periods, groups, metals/nonmetals, lanthanides, Co≠CO). Brief below for consistency if it is ever redrawn.

**Style** (from `_create_more_images.md`): crude, funny, hand-drawn explainer cartoon; stick-figure characters; rough black outlines; mostly white background; selective flat accent colors. Labels, arrows, exaggerated faces, simple metaphors. Minimalist, humorous, concept-first, intentionally rough. Color sparingly: **red** = danger/atomic number emphasis, **yellow** = highlight/warning, **blue** = labels/systems, **green** = metals/organized map, **gray** = messy pile/dull machinery. Vary panel width/height — not every image the same aspect ratio. Ages 11–13; classroom poster vibe OK; no scary gore.

## 081_Periodic_table_04.png — Change protons, change element

**Placement:** End of “Atomic Number” (after paragraph about modern order, before “What ‘Periodic’ Means”).

**Scene:** Three simple atom diagrams in a row:

| Atom | Protons labeled | Element label |
|------|-----------------|---------------|
| 8 protons | 8 (+) in nucleus | **Oxygen** |
| 9 protons | 9 (+) | **Fluorine** |
| 10 protons | 10 (+) | **Neon** |

Big arrow text: **Change protons → change element.** Humor: stick-figure swaps one proton like a LEGO brick and the name tag flips.

**Aspect:** Wide (~2:1).

**Caption idea:** Atomic number counts protons — and defines the element.

# 087. Igneous Rock

## A First Look

Lava cooling after an eruption can become new rock. Deep underground, cooling magma can do the same.

In science, **igneous rock** means **rock formed when molten material cools and solidifies**. The word may seem simple at first, but it gives scientists a precise way to describe patterns in geology.

## The Main Idea

A helpful way to remember igneous rock is to think of it through the phrase **fire-born rock**. That phrase or image is not perfect, but it gives your mind a handle for the concept before the details become more formal.

Igneous rocks form from magma or lava.

Fast cooling can make small crystals or glassy textures; slow cooling can make large crystals.

## Common Misconception

Igneous does not always mean volcanic at the surface; some igneous rocks form underground.

This matters because science rewards careful distinctions. Two ideas can sound similar in ordinary speech but mean different things in science class.

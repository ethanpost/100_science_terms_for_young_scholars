**Style** (from `_create_more_images.md`): crude, funny, hand-drawn explainer cartoon; stick-figure characters; rough black outlines; mostly white background; selective flat accent colors. Labels, arrows, exaggerated faces, simple metaphors. Minimalist, humorous, concept-first, intentionally rough. Color sparingly: **red** = danger/warning, **green** = growth/plants, **blue** = gases/water/systems, **yellow** = sticky-note warnings, **gray** = ash/metal/bureaucracy. Vary panel width/height. Ages 11–13; campfires, candles, bikes, kitchen demos, trees — safe supervision for any experiment.

## 080_Conservation_of_matter_02.png — The candle puzzle

**Placement:** End of “The Candle Puzzle” (after “Matter did not vanish…”).

**Scene:** Tall vertical cross-section of a shrinking candle.

- Liquid wax arrow up wick; label **wax → gases**
- Blue wispy clouds leaving flame: **CO₂**, **H₂O vapor**
- Candle shorter than before; stick-figure: “Wax didn’t vanish — it **left as gas**!”

**Colors:** Orange flame; blue vapor clouds.

**Aspect:** Tall (~1:2).

**Caption idea:** Candle wax becomes invisible gases.

## 080_Conservation_of_matter_03.png — The rust puzzle (mass gain)

**Placement:** End of “The Rust Puzzle”.

**Scene:** Split or before/after.

| Before | After |
|--------|-------|
| Shiny nail on scale: **50 g** | Rusty nail on scale: **53 g** |
| O₂ molecules from air with arrows joining iron | Label: **oxygen from air joined the metal** |

**Humor:** Stick-figure surprised: “It got *heavier*?!” Scientist: “Matter came **in** from outside.”

**Colors:** Gray metal; orange-brown rust; blue O₂.

**Aspect:** Wide (~2:1).

**Caption idea:** Rust can gain mass from oxygen in the air.

## 080_Conservation_of_matter_04.png — Physical change: same stuff, new look

**Placement:** End of “Physical Changes: Same Stuff, New Look”.

**Scene:** Sealed container with ice cubes melting to water.

- Scale or label: **50 g ice** → **50 g water** (same number)
- Thermometer optional; stick-figure: “Still **water** — just liquid now.”

**Colors:** Blue ice/water; no fire.

**Aspect:** Square (~1:1).

**Caption idea:** Melting changes form, not how much matter.

## 080_Conservation_of_matter_05.png — Balanced equation = atom bookkeeping

**Placement:** End of “Chemical Equations and Balancing” (after coefficient vs subscript paragraph).

**Scene:** Equation **2H₂ + O₂ → 2H₂O** with atom counts above/below:

- Left: 4 H, 2 O — Right: 4 H, 2 O
- Checkmarks; stick-figure accountant with ledger: “Every atom accounted for.”

**Humor:** Eraser crossing out a wrongly changed subscript (H₂O₂) with red X — “Don’t change subscripts!”

**Aspect:** Wide banner (~3:1).

**Caption idea:** Balanced equations respect conservation of matter.

## 080_Conservation_of_matter_06.png — Vinegar + baking soda: open vs sealed

**Placement:** End of first kitchen bullet (“Vinegar and baking soda…”).

**Scene:** Two panels, different heights.

| Open cup | Sealed zip bag |
|----------|----------------|
| Bubbles; CO₂ arrows escaping upward | Bag puffed up; gas trapped inside |
| Scale down: **lost mass** | Scale same: **mass unchanged** |
| Label: gas **left** the system | Label: gas still **inside** |

**Colors:** Yellow fizz; blue CO₂ labels.

**Caption idea:** Open cup vs sealed bag — track the gas.

## 080_Conservation_of_matter_07.png — Plants: air becomes tree

**Placement:** End of “Plants” bullet under “Fire, Food, and Growing Things”.

**Scene:** Tree with green leaves; arrows from blue **CO₂** clouds in air and blue **H₂O** from soil into trunk.

- Label: **carbon from air → wood & leaves**
- Stick-figure: “The tree ate **air**?!” Scientist: “Air is **matter**.”

**Colors:** Green tree; blue CO₂/H₂O.

**Aspect:** Tall (~1:1.5).

**Caption idea:** Much of a plant’s mass comes from CO₂ in the air.

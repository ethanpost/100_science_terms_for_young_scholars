# Image briefs — 083 Sun

Use when creating `083_Sun_02.png` through `083_Sun_08.png`. Each file is referenced in `083_Sun.md` at the placement noted below.

`083_Sun_01.png` already exists at the chapter top. Brief below for consistency if it is ever redrawn.

**Style** (from `_create_more_images.md`): crude, funny, hand-drawn explainer cartoon; stick-figure characters; rough black outlines; mostly white background; selective flat accent colors. Labels, arrows, exaggerated faces, simple metaphors. Minimalist, humorous, concept-first, intentionally rough. Color sparingly: **red** = danger, **yellow/orange** = Sun/heat/warning, **blue** = sky/water/Earth, **green** = plants/growth, **gray** = dull machinery. Vary panel width/height. Ages 11–13; sports/outdoor scenes OK. **Never** show staring at the Sun without protection — eclipse safety images must show certified glasses or pinhole projection.

## 083_Sun_02.png — The Sun is a star (distance)

**Placement:** End of “The Sun Is a Star” (before “The Boss of the Solar System”).

**Scene:** Split panel. Left: stick-figure kid at night, tiny dots in sky labeled **other stars**. Right: same kid squinting at huge yellow Sun labeled **our star — close!** Arrow between: **distance**, not different kind of object.

**Humor:** Night stick figure: “Tiny.” Day stick figure with tiny sunglasses: “Huge. Same thing.”

**Aspect:** Wide (~2:1).

**Caption idea:** The Sun is a star — it only looks bigger because it is close.

---

## 083_Sun_03.png — Boss of the solar system (mass + gravity)

**Placement:** End of “The Boss of the Solar System” (after the role table).

**Scene:** Huge yellow Sun in center with label **~99.8% of solar system mass**. Tiny planets on orbit paths (Earth, Jupiter dots OK). Stick-figure Earth on string metaphor: **gravity** arrow toward Sun. Text: **not because it’s bright — because it’s massive**.

**Aspect:** Square or wide.

**Caption idea:** The Sun’s mass and gravity hold the solar system together.

---

## 083_Sun_04.png — Not combustion — nuclear fusion

**Placement:** End of “The Sun Is Not ‘On Fire’” (after fusion paragraph, before “Inside the Sun”).

**Scene:** Two panels. Left: campfire with red **X**, labels **needs oxygen**, **chemical burn**, **hours**. Right: Sun cutaway core with **H → He**, label **nuclear fusion**, **no oxygen**, **billions of years**. Stick-figure shivering then warmed by sunlight: “Energy from the **core**, not a space campfire.”

**Aspect:** Wide (~2:1).

**Caption idea:** The Sun shines by fusion, not combustion.

---

## 083_Sun_05.png — Layers of the Sun (energy’s slow escape)

**Placement:** End of “Inside the Sun: Layers of a Fusion Engine” (after corona paragraph).

**Scene:** Vertical cutaway Sun. Labeled bands: **CORE** (fusion), **radiative zone** (zigzag photon labeled “bounce for ages”), **convective zone** (arrows up/down like soup), **photosphere**, **chromosphere** (thin red rim), **corona** (wispy blue). Optional grainy photosphere texture.

**Humor:** Stick-figure photon dizzy: “Still not out yet!”

**Aspect:** Tall vertical (~3:4).

**Caption idea:** Energy is born in the core and slowly works its way out.

---

## 083_Sun_06.png — Sunlight: eight minutes to Earth

**Placement:** End of “Sunlight: Eight Minutes from Star to Skin”.

**Scene:** Sun on left, Earth on right, wavy yellow arrow between labeled **~8 minutes** at speed of light. Stick-figure on Earth checking watch: “This sunshine is **8 minutes old**.” Small icons: visible light (eye), infrared (heat waves), UV (sunscreen bottle, yellow warning).

**Aspect:** Wide horizontal.

**Caption idea:** Sunlight takes about eight minutes to reach Earth.

---

## 083_Sun_07.png — How the Sun runs Earth (three systems)

**Placement:** End of “How the Sun Runs Earth” (before “Size and Gravity”).

**Scene:** Three mini-panels in one image (unequal sizes OK).

1. **Water cycle:** Sun over ocean, arrow up **evaporate**, cloud, rain.
2. **Food chain:** Sun → plant → burger on plate; label **photosynthesis → your lunch**.
3. **Seasons:** Earth on orbit, axis **tilted**; one hemisphere toward Sun labeled **summer (tilted toward)**, other away labeled **winter (tilted away)**. Small note: **not mainly closer/farther**.

**Aspect:** Wide (~2:1) or tall stacked.

**Caption idea:** The Sun drives weather, the water cycle, food chains, and seasons.

---

## 083_Sun_08.png — Space weather and safe viewing

**Placement:** End of “Eclipses and Safe Viewing” (after safety rules list).

**Scene:** Top half: cartoon Sun with **sunspot**, **solar flare** burst, **CME** blob heading toward Earth; Earth’s magnetosphere deflecting some; **aurora** green curtain at pole. Bottom half: eclipse with Moon blocking Sun; stick-figure wearing **certified eclipse glasses** (not sunglasses); crossed-out eye staring at Sun with red **DON’T**.

**Labels:** space weather; **use proper protection**.

**Aspect:** Wide (~2:1).

**Caption idea:** The active Sun affects Earth — and you must observe it safely.

# 077. Nitrogen Cycle

## A First Look

Most of the air is nitrogen, but plants cannot use that nitrogen gas directly. The nitrogen cycle changes it into usable forms.

In science, **nitrogen cycle** means **the movement of nitrogen through the atmosphere, soil, living things, and water**. The word may seem simple at first, but it gives scientists a precise way to describe patterns in Earth and life science.

## The Main Idea

A helpful way to remember nitrogen cycle is to think of it through the phrase **nitrogen recycling**. That phrase or image is not perfect, but it gives your mind a handle for the concept before the details become more formal.

Nitrogen fixation changes nitrogen gas into forms organisms can use.

Decomposition and bacteria return nitrogen to soil and atmosphere.

## Common Misconception

Nitrogen is not useful to most plants just because it is in the air; it must be chemically converted.

This matters because science rewards careful distinctions. Two ideas can sound similar in ordinary speech but mean different things in science class.

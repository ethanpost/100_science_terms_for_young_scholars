# Teacher's Guide

## 098. Equator

### Learning Goals

Students should be able to:

- Define the **equator** as **0° latitude**, a **great circle** midway between poles.
- Explain **Northern** vs **Southern Hemisphere** division.
- Describe equatorial **day-length** patterns compared with high latitudes (qualitative).
- Introduce **celestial equator** as the sky projection of Earth's equator.
- Distinguish equator (latitude reference) from **prime meridian** (longitude reference).
- Reject common myths (e.g., reliable sink spiral direction) with careful reasoning.

### Answers to Study Questions

1. The **equator** is Earth's **0° latitude** line, dividing Earth into northern and southern halves.
2. **0°**.
3. **Northern Hemisphere** and **Southern Hemisphere**.
4. Its plane passes through **Earth's center**, making it a **great circle**, the largest possible circle on Earth's surface.
5. Near the equator, day length stays near **12 hours** year-round; high latitudes have strong seasonal changes in day length.
6. The **celestial equator** is Earth's equator projected outward onto the **sky**, useful for describing star paths.
7. The equator is **latitude 0** (north-south reference). The prime meridian is **longitude 0** (east-west reference).
8. Accept reasonable answers, e.g., "Equator always hottest spot on Earth at every instant" is not reliably true due to **weather** and geography.

### Activity Suggestions

1. **Globe Hemisphere Paint**
   Color hemispheres differently and trace equator as the boundary.

2. **Daylight Hours Compare**
   Use tables for Quito vs Reykjavik across months; students summarize patterns.

3. **Great Circle String**
   Demonstrate that only equator among parallels is a great circle using a ball and string through center.

4. **Celestial Sphere Model**
   Use a dome grid or planetarium software to show celestial equator vs paths of Sun and planets.

5. **Myth Investigation: Coriolis at Sink Scale**
   Discuss scale, turbulence, and why demonstrations are misleading without controlled tanks.

6. **Equatorial Geography Tour**
   Name countries the equator crosses; map mountains/rainforests/deserts to show diversity.

7. **Speed at Surface Calculation (Enrichment)**
   Compute equatorial circumference divided by 24 h for surface speed (conceptual algebra).

8. **Latitude Sign Drill**
   Given cities, identify N/S relative to equator using maps only.

### Teaching Notes

- Emphasize **great circle** concept as foundation for later navigation and aviation great-circle routes.
- Avoid overstating uniform climate; rainfall and elevation matter.
- Pair with **poles** and **latitude** chapters for geometry coherence.
- Sink Coriolis myth: handle gently to teach critical thinking without shaming.

# 069. Predator

## A First Look

A hawk diving after a mouse is acting as a predator. Predators are important parts of balanced ecosystems.

In science, **predator** means **an organism that hunts, kills, and eats other organisms**. The word may seem simple at first, but it gives scientists a precise way to describe patterns in ecology.

## The Main Idea

A helpful way to remember predator is to think of it through the phrase **hunter**. That phrase or image is not perfect, but it gives your mind a handle for the concept before the details become more formal.

Predators obtain energy by eating prey.

Predators can influence prey behavior, population size, and evolution.

## Why Scientists Care

Scientists care about predator because it helps them explain evidence rather than merely name it. A strong science student asks: What can I observe? What pattern do I notice? What explanation fits the evidence? This term helps answer those questions.

In modern science, predator also connects to tools and decisions. Researchers may measure it directly, build models of it, compare examples of it, or use it to solve practical problems. Engineers, doctors, environmental scientists, astronomers, and chemists all depend on clear terms like this one.

## A Closer Look

To understand predator, focus on relationships. Ask what parts are involved, how those parts interact, and what changes when conditions change. Many science mistakes happen when students memorize a definition but cannot apply it to a new situation.

## Common Misconception

Predators are not simply villains; they help keep ecosystems functioning.

This matters because science rewards careful distinctions. Two ideas can sound similar in ordinary speech but mean different things in science class.
